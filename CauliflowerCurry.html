Cauliflower Potato Vegan Rendang
<
Recipe Type: Main
Cuisine: Malaysian
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 50 minutes
Yeild: Serves 2-3
Dietary Requirement: Vegan, Gluten Free
Ingredients
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 3 medium potatoes - cut into 2cm chunks
  • 1/2 Cauliflower - cut into 3-4cm chunks
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 1 tsp coconut sugar
  • sea salt
  • pepper
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 3 lime leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • lime wedges abd coriander leaves to serve

    • Spice paste:
  • 4 shallots - peel and roughly chop
  • 4 gloves garlic, peel
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, roughly chop
  • 1 inch piece fresh galangag - peel and roughly chop
  • 1 inch piece fresh ginger (2 if you do not have galanga) - peel and roughly chop
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 red chillies - seed in and roughly chop
  • 10 coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil
Now, Let's cook ;)
  1. Spice paste:Put all the ingredients into a small processor or blender and blitz until it is smooth. The ideal time for the paste is a week; however, you can still use it straigh away. The longer you keep, the better it is.

  2. Boil a big pot of hot water, put cauliflower and potatos and turn off the heat immediately. Stir them for 30 seconds then rinse with cold water. Then let them dry out a bit.

  3. Heat the oil in a large pan, add the paste and stir it for 1 minute (or until you can feel the smell)

  4. Add bay leave, lime leave and cinnamon stick, stir it. You can add some water if feel it is too dry.

  5. Add cauliflower and potatos, stir it evenly then pour the coconut milk, flavour it with sugar and salt, bring to simmer, cover with the lid them cook for 20 minutes.

  6. I love to add some more turmeric during cooking as it is really good for digestion.
Serve:Put some rice in a bowl with the rendang on the top and scatter over with coriander leaves and pepper just as you want.
